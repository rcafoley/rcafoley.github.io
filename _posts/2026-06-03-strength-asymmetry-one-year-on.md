Time flies. It's been one year since the publication of our [meta-analysis on upper limb strength asymmetry](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-025-87413-w) in *Scientific Reports*, and it has been exciting to see the feedback and citations roll in across so many disciplines.

To name a few highlights: it was referenced in a *New York Times* article on daily wellness; [Nicholas La Delfa](https://www.linkedin.com/in/nicholas-la-delfa-b2223337/) and I were guests on a criminal justice podcast to discuss forensic biomechanics (*Undisclosed: Towards Justice*, S7:E4); and it has picked up citations in journals ranging from *Psychological Research* to *Sensors*, *Brain Sciences*, *Modern Rheumatology*, and the *Journal of Electromyography & Kinesiology*.

It is eye-opening: this research was conceived with ergonomics use-cases in mind, yet it has proven to have so much interdisciplinary utility.

Thank you again to all the authors on a heavy lift that spanned almost five years - [Garrick Forman](https://www.linkedin.com/in/garrickforman/), Jeffrey Graham, [Mike Holmes](https://www.linkedin.com/in/mike-holmes-6839ba18/), and [Nicholas La Delfa](https://www.linkedin.com/in/nicholas-la-delfa-b2223337/).
